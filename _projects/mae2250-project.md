# MAE 2250 Project

## Project Overview
This project focuses on the issue of spotted lanternfly (SLF) infestations in vineyards. We must create a design to prevent SLF damaging crops and reducing fruit quality. 
Our team developed a device that attracts SLFs using a 60HZ vibration frequency and zaps them using an electric mesh.

## Client Pitch (O3)

### Problem
Spotted lanternflies significantly damage grapevines and reduce fruit yield and quality. Current solutions rely heavily on pesticides which risks contaminating grapes and harms the environment.

### Proposed Solution
We proposed a non-chemical device that attracts SLFs using vibration and eliminates them by zapping them. This reduces SLF interaction with crops and leads to tastier produce and higher yield.

### Key Features
- Uses 60 Hz vibration to attract SLFs  
- Eliminates SLFs using an electric mesh system  
- Reduces pesticide use and crop contamination

## Functional Prototype (O5)

### Description
We developed a working prototype consisting of a vibrating pole mounted on a 3D-printed base containing a motor and a wooden striker that hit the pole to create vibrations.

### How It Works
- A DC motor powers up
- The cam attached to the motor strikes the pole
- Audio vibrations of 25Hz are produced
- The pole spins as a result of the cam striking it

### Build Process
- Designed base plate in CAD and 3D printed it
- Attached a pole in the center of the base
- Used tape to hold down motor and placed next to pole
- Attached a wooden striker to the motor
- Switched on the motor so that the striker could hit the pole

## Client Report

### Final Solution
The final design is a **Vibrating Zapper Column** that attracts and eliminates SLFs without using chemicals. Compared to the initial concept, the system was refined to achieve the target 60 Hz vibration and improved durability through better materials and design adjustments.

The final design is a vibrating zapper column that attracts and eliminates SLF using vibration and zapping. Compared to the inital concept, the product was refined using gear ratios to achieve the target 60 Hz vibration.

### How It Works / How to Use
1. Place the device between vineyard rows  
2. Power the system by switching on the button placed on the battery box
3. The pole vibrates at 60 Hz to attract SLFs  
4. SLFs contact the electric mesh and are zapped

### Results and Testing
- Achieved target 60 Hz vibration frequency
- Operated continuously for 5 minutes without failure
- Withstood 10 N lateral force without deformation which shows structural stability  

These results confirm that the device meets functional, durability, and stability requirements.

### Conclusion
The device demonstrates a pesticide-free alternative that successfully reduces SLF interaction with grapevines and protects the produce quality and yield.

### Next Steps
- Incorporate weather resistance materials for outdoor use  
- Integrate battery or solar power for zapping mechanism
- Test device effectiveness at the larger vineyard scale
